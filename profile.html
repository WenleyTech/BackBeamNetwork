Profile statement

You don’t have to explain everything from the beginning.

I work with clients who have spent years carrying more than they should — financially, emotionally, or both — and help them make sense of the pressure, guilt, and boundary strain that often comes with being the dependable one.

My work is especially relevant for clients navigating family financial obligation, financial betrayal, and the emotional aftermath of always being expected to hold things together.

Support should not come at the cost of your own stability.

Approach

How this work is framed

Therapeutic orientation
Trauma-informed care, family systems, relational therapy, and boundary-focused work.
What this may help with
Clarifying patterns, reducing guilt, rebuilding trust, and creating healthier financial and relational boundaries.
Session style
Warm, direct, reflective, and structured — designed to help clients name what has happened and move forward more clearly.
Who this may be a fit for
Adults navigating family pressure, relationship imbalance, betrayal, or long-standing responsibility patterns connected to money.
What to expect

A clearer, calmer starting point

01
Start with what feels true
You do not need to arrive with the perfect label. Sessions can begin with the situation itself — pressure, guilt, betrayal, or exhaustion.
02
Name the pattern
The work often involves identifying how money, loyalty, trust, and obligation have shaped the dynamic.
03
Build more agency
The goal is not just insight — it is more clarity, stronger limits, and a steadier sense of what is and is not yours to carry.
